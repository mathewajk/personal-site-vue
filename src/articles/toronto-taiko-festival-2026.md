## August 9th, 2026: Workshop

### Tokyo Shishimai (Lion Dance) - History and Demonstration

Experience the rhythms and movements of Tokyo performing arts through Edo Kotobuki Jishi, the traditional celebratory shishimai (lion dance) of Tokyo. Through live demonstration and conversation, participants will have the chance to experience Edo Kotobuki Jishi in the style of premiere performing group Wakayama Shachū, designated a Nationally Important Intangible Folk Cultural Asset by the government of Japan. Together with special guest Eien Hunter-Ishikawa, we will explore this art form's history and context, how it continues to evolve, and how the study of traditional arts can inform our approaches to taiko more broadly. We will also take a closer look at the specific instruments used by the ensemble, including the shimedaiko (high-pitched taiko), okedo daiko (low-pitched taiko), shinobue (bamboo flute), and kane (handheld gong). No prior taiko experience is required.

#### Facilitators

**Eien Hunter-Ishikawa** is a musician, educator, and composer specializing in taiko, shinobue, percussion, and shishimai. Recognized for his musicianship and versatility, he integrates his training in jazz, Western percussion, and traditional Japanese music for a distinctive and refined approach to instruction and performance. Eien is a passionate advocate of Edo Bayashi and Kotobuki Jishi and has studied for over twenty years with Kyosuke Suzuki of Wakayama Taneo Shachū, a professional ensemble holding the distinction of Nationally Important Intangible Folk Cultural Asset. Eien is the founder of Portland Shishimai Kai, a group dedicated to the preservation and expansion of Edo Kotobuki Jishi by offering performances and workshops around North America and beyond.

[eienhunterishikawa.com](https://eienhunterishikawa.com)

**Mathew Jacqmin-Kramer** is a long-time taiko player and recent transplant to Toronto. He combines a background in classical piano and composition for interactive media with study of traditional Japanese music through koto and shishimai to bring a multifaceted perspective to taiko performance and composition. As a member of the Great Lakes Taiko Center, he has performed with multiple groups, including the Godaiko Drummers, Raion Taiko, Sakura Japanese Instrumental Group, and his own taiko group, Sazanami Taiko Arts Ensemble. He began studying Edo Kotobuki Jishi with Eien Hunter-Ishikawa in 2024 and recently founded Toronto Shishimai Kai with the goal of bringing the study and performance of the Wakayama-ryū repertoire to the GTA.
